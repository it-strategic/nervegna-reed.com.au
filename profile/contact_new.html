profile

Nervegna Reed Architecture is an award-winning architecture practice established in Melbourne in 2004, working on projects across a broad range of scales and categories. We focus on design methodology which is place based for a unique cultural and program experience. Each NR design is a bespoke response to the site, program and client expectations. NR’s recent design for the Central Goldfields Art Gallery was exhibited in the Australian Pavilion at the 2023 Venice Architecture Biennale, published in ArchDaily and other local and international publications. The project recently won the AIA Victorian Chapter’s highest architecture award for Heritage Architecture, the John George Knight Award for Heritage Architecture. Integral to the design of the gallery was the Indigenous engagement and collaboration with Djandak and Djaara Members for the Interpretive Indigenous Garden, which is integrally linked to the art galley through specific views and spatial channels. This collaboration was awarded in the LGPro Award for Excellence in the First Nations Community Partnership category. Other NR projects include large-scale master planning for Nanjing and Dazhou in China, religious spaces for the Victorian Quakers Centre (included in Open House culture programs) and social housing and community projects in regional Victoria, Hobart and Melbourne. Currently NR are building the new Shepparton Club, which will be an iconic focal point for Shepparton in regional Victoria. NR’s micro gallery / studio, the Arrow Studio near Woodend was included in Phaidon’s ‘Nanotecture’ and the ‘Phaidon Atlas of Contemporary World Architecture’ (web edition). NR value sustainability and NR’s PEP Dandenong (Precinct Energy Project) led the way in Australian architecture and urbanism for green district energy, being the first precinct in Australia to be powered by co-generation.

NR also has vast experience with gallery and architectural video installations for the RMIT Gallery, RMIT Design Hub, MUMA, the MPavilion and the Anna Pappas Gallery. Anna Nervegna has experience in art installations such as The Expanded Field with Callum Morton, and Danius Kesminas. In 2019 NR led the University of Melbourne exhibit in the Chambord Inachevé exhibition curated by Dominique Perrault, in Chateau Chambord, France. NR extend their architectural dialogue with writings and the making of architectural documentaries and video installations for exhibitions, such as the ‘Future Happiness’ video installation for the ‘Occupied’ exhibition at RMIT Design Hub in collaboration with Callum Morton. In 2018 NR guest edited the Architecture Victoria special issue ‘The Return of the Object’ and in 2022 guest edited the special issues of 2A Magazine, ‘Australia – the World in Reverse’. NR’s documentaries ‘The Architecture of Edmond and Corrigan’ and ‘Peter + Dione McIntyre – Counterbalancing Forces’ both won the AIA Bates Smart Media award (2014 and 2024) and were exhibited at RMIT Gallery and the MPavilion. Recently a chapter from Toby Reed’s 2018 PhD (RMIT University) has been published in ‘The Physical and the Digital City’ edited by Silvio Carta (Graham Cairns, series editor).
